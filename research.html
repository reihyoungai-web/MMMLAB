Research

From marine microbiomes and invertebrate symbionts to biosynthetic gene clusters and new bioactive natural products.

Marine natural products & chemical ecology

Isolation and structure elucidation of bioactive marine natural products from invertebrates and associated microorganisms, with a focus on antibacterial, antifungal, and anticancer activities.

  • Chromatographic purification and structural analysis (LC-MS, NMR)
  • Bioassay-guided fractionation and activity profiling
  • Chemical ecology between hosts and symbionts

Marine microbial diversity & symbiosis

Cultivation and characterization of marine-derived bacteria and fungi from diverse environments including sediments, invertebrates, and coastal ecosystems.

  • Isolation of symbiotic actinomycetes and fungi
  • Optimization of culture media for secondary metabolite production
  • Microbial community analysis in environmental samples

Biosynthetic gene clusters & engineering

Discovery and engineering of biosynthetic gene clusters (BGCs) for marine natural products using modern genetic tools.

  • Cloning BGCs and heterologous expression in Streptomyces
  • CRISPR/Cas-based genome engineering
  • Promoter engineering to enhance metabolite production

Metagenomics & bioinformatics

Mining metagenomic datasets to uncover cryptic biosynthetic potential and explore microbial community structures.

  • Analysis of metagenomic sequencing data
  • Sequence-based prediction of secondary metabolite pathways
  • Diversity assessment of environmental microbiomes
